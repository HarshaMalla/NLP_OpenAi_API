“Making Food History”: fourth-graders at the Trinity School in St. Louis take part in All Souls 2016’s Food Heritage Festival and teach visitors about the culture of black Americans. (Photo: Jamison Cave.)



The Sutton Place & Roof Terrace Garden’s vegetable beds include 20 varieties of tomatoes and 18 kinds of beans, which are planted with care by All Souls participants. For the 2016 Food History Festival, the garden has designed a tasting garden’s 12 different herbs, fennel, chard, peppers, skeen beans, collard greens, and more.
